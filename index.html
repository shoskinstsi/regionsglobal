2. Closing Questionnaire

Use these questions as a guided debrief with the client, internal pursuit team, or sales owner.

Decision & Selection

Understand why the client made their final decision

Capture the decision story in a consistent way so North America Sales, Marketing, and Leadership can identify trends.

Why did you choose CTM?Document the strongest reasons CTM was selected, including service, technology, relationship, pricing, global alignment, implementation confidence, or overall value.
Which supplier or competitor did you choose?Record whether CTM was selected or identify the selected supplier / competitor for future comparison and competitive tracking.
What were the top factors that influenced the final decision?Capture the primary decision drivers, buying committee priorities, must-have requirements, and deal-breakers.
Was there anything CTM did especially well?Highlight strengths, differentiators, team actions, proposal elements, presentations, or relationship points that helped the pursuit.
Was there anything CTM could have done differently?Identify improvement opportunities, missed expectations, unclear messaging, timing issues, gaps, or additional support needed.
Proposal & Presentation

Review the quality and clarity of CTM’s response

Use these questions to understand whether the proposal and presentation clearly addressed the client’s needs and supported the final decision.

How well was the proposal prepared?Rate quality, accuracy, organization, professionalism, completeness, and readiness of the final response.
Did the proposal clearly address business needs and priorities?Confirm whether CTM connected the solution to the client’s goals, pain points, regional needs, and decision criteria.
Was pricing, service model, and value proposition clear?Assess whether the commercial model, support structure, implementation approach, and value story were easy to understand.
Did the presentation provide enough detail?Capture feedback on presentation depth, storytelling, audience alignment, executive messaging, and supporting proof points.
Were there gaps in the proposal or presentation?Document missing information, unclear sections, unanswered questions, weak proof points, or topics requiring follow-up.
Tip: Ask for specific examples whenever possible. Specific feedback is easier to turn into proposal improvements, battlecard updates, coaching, and pursuit planning actions.

Decision & Selection

Understand why the client made their final decision

Review the quality and clarity of CTM’s response

How well was the proposal prepared?Rate the quality, accuracy, organization, professionalism, completeness, and readiness of the final response.
